## Section 6 — FAQ (pricing-specific)

**Section heading (H2):**
Questions about pricing.

**Body (6 Qs, 40-60 words each):**

- **Q: Is this really the full cost, or will there be add-ons?**
  A: The package price is final for the scope listed. Add-ons (extra photoshoot day, extra blog posts, custom integrations) are only added if you explicitly ask. Our proposal spells them out line-by-line before you sign anything.

- **Q: Can I pay in installments?**
  A: Yes. 50% to start, 50% at launch is standard. If cashflow is tight, we'll do 3 equal payments across the project at no markup. Just ask on the intro call.

- **Q: What if I already have a logo / photos / copy?**
  A: Great, we'll use them. We reduce the package price if your materials save us meaningful time — usually $200–$500 depending on what you bring. No one is overpaying for stuff they already own.

- **Q: What's the actual refund policy?**
  A: Before Gate 2 (wireframe approval): full refund minus any hours already billed at $90/hr. After Gate 2: the 50% deposit is non-refundable because we've already scheduled design+dev time. We've never had to refund anyone, but the policy's in writing so you know what it is.

- **Q: Why no "starting at" pricing?**
  A: Because "starting at $X" always means "actually $3X after discovery." We'd rather publish the real number. If your scope is weirder than our three packages, we'll quote custom before kickoff.

- **Q: What if I just want a small fix to my existing site?**
  A: Skip the packages. The care plan is $125/month and covers an hour of fixes. Most small-fix clients just need that. Email rylan@handsoffdesign.com with the request.

## Anti-patterns we're not shipping

- "Contact us for pricing" — the whole point of this page is numbers.
- "Each project is unique, so pricing varies" — it does, but we still publish the three packages.
- "Investment" as a synonym for "cost" — it's cost, say cost.
- "Premium," "Enterprise," "Bespoke" — we're a small shop for small shops.
- Fake urgency ("Only 2 spots left this month!") — if we have capacity we take it, and we're never going to pressure-sell.
