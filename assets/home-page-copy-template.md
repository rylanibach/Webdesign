# Home Page Copy Template — Phoenix Small Business

**Owner:** Raul | **Pairs with:** `voice-and-copy-guide.md`, `services-page-copy-template.md`, `about-page-copy-template.md`, `faq-copy-template.md`

The home page is the handshake. Most Phoenix homeowners will decide in 4–8 seconds whether you're "the real local guy" or "some out-of-town outfit." This template gives us the 6 sections every home page needs, with fill-in blanks, anti-patterns we refuse to ship, and per-industry archetypes.

---

## The 6 sections (in order)

1. **Hero** — headline, subhead, primary CTA, trust whisper
2. **Trust bar** — 4–6 proof signals under the hero fold
3. **Services teaser** — 3 tiles, one per core service, linking to detail
4. **Why us** — 3 differentiator cards with a number in each
5. **Social proof** — 2–3 real reviews + rating snapshot
6. **Bottom CTA band** — one question, one button, one phone number

A Phoenix small-biz home page should be **under 800 words** total. If we're over, we're padding.

---

## Section 1 — Hero

### Structure
- **Eyebrow** (optional): `[CITY/AREA SERVED] · [CATEGORY]` — e.g., "Gilbert, AZ · Window Cleaning"
- **Headline** (H1): 4–9 words. States the outcome, not the service.
- **Subhead**: 12–22 words. Names the customer + the problem + the promise.
- **Primary CTA button**: verb + outcome. Never "Submit."
- **Secondary CTA** (optional): phone number as text link (mobile = tap-to-call).
- **Trust whisper**: one line under CTAs — e.g., "Licensed AZ ROC #xxxxxx · 15 years in the Valley"

### Headline archetypes (pick one)

**Outcome** — "Your yard, finally handled."
**Pain-relief** — "Stop waiting 3 days for a plumber."
**Local-authority** — "The Phoenix [service] Yelp keeps recommending."
**Specific-promise** — "Same-day appliance repair across Phoenix."
**Question** — "Broken garage door? We're already on the way."

### Subhead archetypes

**Who + what + difference**
> "We do [service] for Phoenix homeowners who want the job done right the first time — no upsells, no subcontractors."

**Pain + fix**
> "Bad landscaping makes every other part of your house look worse. We fix that in one visit."

**Proof + promise**
> "[N] reviews. [N] years. Family-owned. And we answer the phone."

### Anti-patterns (never ship)
- "Welcome to [Business Name]" — wastes the most valuable real estate on the site
- "We are dedicated to providing quality service..." — generic, dead, corporate
- "#1 [Service] in Phoenix" — unprovable; homeowners distrust it immediately
- Headlines over 10 words
- Subheads that don't name a specific person or problem

### Fill-in template

```
Eyebrow:   [CITY] · [INDUSTRY]
Headline:  [VERB] [OUTCOME] — [PHOENIX TWIST]
Subhead:   [AUDIENCE] who [PAIN] get [PROMISE] from [BUSINESS].
CTA 1:     [VERB] [OUTCOME]  →  /contact or scroll to form
CTA 2:     [PHONE] (tap to call)
Trust:     [CREDENTIAL] · [YEARS] in the Valley
```

## Section 2 — Trust bar

One horizontal row (mobile: 2×3 grid). No copy beyond 2–5 words per tile.

Pick 4–6 from this menu based on what the client has:

| Signal | Example |
|---|---|
| License / ROC | "AZ ROC #316471" |
| Years | "15 years in Phoenix" |
| Reviews | "⭐ 4.9 · 87 reviews" |
| Service area | "Serving Phoenix, Scottsdale, Tempe" |
| Guarantee | "Same-day available" |
| Ownership | "Family-owned" |
| Hours | "Open 7 days" |
| Insurance | "Licensed · Bonded · Insured" |
| Response time | "30-minute callback" |
| Payment | "No-contract pricing" |

Do not invent any of these. If the client can't prove it, cut it.

## Section 3 — Services teaser

3 cards, 1 per top service. Each card:
- **Service name** (bolded — e.g., "Drain Cleaning")
- **One-line description** (10–18 words — what it is + why they need it)
- **"Starts at $XXX"** if client will commit to pricing; else "Free quote"
- **Link** to the services detail page

### Example (plumbing)

> **Emergency repairs**
> Water heater, burst pipe, or stubborn clog — we answer the phone and we're usually there in under 60 minutes.
> [Call now →]

> **Drain & sewer**
> Scope cameras, hydro jetting, and the full-line replacement when it's time. No more Drain-o bandaids.
> [Get a quote →]

> **Fixture install**
> Toilets, sinks, disposals, water softeners. Brought by us or supplied by you — we don't upsell fixtures.
> [See pricing →]

## Section 4 — Why us (differentiators)

3 cards. Every card must have a **number** in it — numbers beat adjectives every time.

### Archetypes

- **Experience**: "20 years working Phoenix homes"
- **Response**: "Most calls answered in under 2 rings"
- **Price**: "Flat rates published up front — no $89 'diagnostic fees'"
- **Ownership**: "The owner does every estimate, not a salesperson"
- **Guarantee**: "2-year workmanship guarantee, in writing"
- **Coverage**: "30-mile service radius from central Phoenix"

Each card:
- **Headline** (3–7 words) — the promise
- **Supporting line** (15–25 words) — why it matters to the customer

### Example

> **We answer on the first ring.**
> When your AC dies at 4pm in July, you don't have time to leave a voicemail. One of our three dispatchers picks up, every time.

## Section 5 — Social proof

Two acceptable shapes:

**Shape A — Review quote strip** (preferred)
- 2–3 verbatim Google/Yelp reviews
- Name + first letter of last name + neighborhood/city
- Star row above each
- Small "Read all [N] reviews on Google →" link at end

**Shape B — Stats + photo**
- Big number ("4.9 ⭐ across 87 reviews")
- Photo of owner or crew
- One quote pulled
- "Read them on Google" link

Never fabricate reviews. Never edit quotes beyond light punctuation. Always get client permission before pulling from Yelp.

## Section 6 — Bottom CTA band

One line. One button. One phone number.

### Fill-in

```
[H2 HEADLINE — ONE QUESTION OR STATEMENT]
[OPTIONAL SUBLINE — 10 WORDS MAX]

[PRIMARY CTA BUTTON]     [PHONE NUMBER — TAP TO CALL]
```

### Examples

**Service-oriented**
> Ready for a quote?
> Most estimates are back to you same day.
> [Request a free quote]    (480) 810-4870

**Urgency-oriented**
> Got a plumbing emergency?
> We'll be there within the hour.
> [Call now — (602) xxx-xxxx]

**Trust-oriented**
> Let's see if we're the right fit.
> 15-minute call, zero pressure.
> [Book a call]    (602) xxx-xxxx

## Voice rules (hard stops)

**Do:**
- Use contractions ("we'll," "don't," "you're") always
- Write at a 7th-grade reading level
- Use Phoenix names: Valley, Maricopa, Ahwatukee, West Valley, East Valley, Sonoran
- Lead with benefit, then capability
- Put the phone number in at least 4 places on the page

**Don't:**
- "Dedicated to excellence"
- "Your trusted partner"
- "Best-in-class"
- "At [Business Name], we believe..."
- "For all your [service] needs"
- Any sentence over 22 words
