# Voice & Copy Guide — Phoenix Small Business Websites

Reusable guide for writing website copy for our clients. Copy to `clients/<name>/content/voice.md` and customize per client.

---

## Tone

- **Warm and direct.** Talk like a neighbor, not a corporation.
- **Confident but not pushy.** We know our stuff — no need to oversell.
- **Phoenix-local.** Reference the heat, the Valley, specific cities (Scottsdale, Tempe, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert) when it fits naturally. Don't force it.
- **Benefit-led.** Lead with what the customer gets, not what the business does.

## Standard Page Sections

### Hero Headline + Subhead
- Headline: 6–10 words. State the core benefit or identity.
  - Good: "Keeping Phoenix Homes Cool Since 2005"
  - Bad: "Welcome to Our Website"
- Subhead: 1 sentence. Expand on the headline with a specific detail.
  - Good: "Licensed, insured HVAC service across the East Valley — same-day appointments available."
  - Bad: "We offer a variety of services to meet your needs."

### Value Props (3 blocks)
- Pick the client's top 3 differentiators. Each gets:
  - A short title (3–5 words)
  - A 1–2 sentence explanation
  - Example: "Same-Day Service" / "Call before noon, we're there today. No waiting around in the Arizona heat."

### About Section
- 2–3 sentences. Who they are, how long they've been at it, why they care.
- Include: years in business, family-owned (if true), license/certification, service area.
- Humanize: mention the owner's name, a personal detail if available.

### Testimonials
- Pull from Google Reviews or Yelp (with permission).
- Include first name + city: "— Maria, Gilbert"
- Keep to 1–3 sentences each.

### CTA Blocks
- Primary CTA: "Get a Free Quote" or "Schedule Your Free Audit"
- Always include phone number next to the button.
- Repeat the CTA at least twice on the home page (hero + bottom band).

## Words to Avoid
- "Synergy," "leverage," "solutions," "utilize," "cutting-edge"
- "Click here" (use descriptive link text)
- Superlatives without proof ("best in Phoenix" — unless they have an award)

## Words to Use
- "Your," "you" (talk to the customer, not about the business)
- Specific numbers ("23 years," "500+ homes served," "4.9 stars")
- Local references ("Valley-wide," "East Valley," "right here in Phoenix")

## Industry-Specific Hooks

Use these as starting points when drafting hero headlines and CTAs for specific industries:

| Industry | Hero Angle | CTA Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Landscaping | "Your yard, but better" / desert-friendly curb appeal | Free yard consultation |
| HVAC / Plumbing | Urgency + reliability — "don't sweat it" | Same-day service scheduling |
| Auto Repair (Mobile) | Convenience — "we come to you" | Book a mobile appointment |
| Pet Grooming (Mobile) | Stress-free for pets — "grooming at your door" | Schedule a visit |
| Fencing / Concrete | Durability + pride — "built to last in the Arizona sun" | Free on-site estimate |
| Painting | Transformation — "fresh look, same home" | Free color consultation |
| Handyman | One call, everything handled | Free walkthrough estimate |
| Pool Service | Crystal clear, no hassle | Free pool inspection |
| Pressure Washing | Instant curb appeal | Before/after photo + free quote |

## Formatting
- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max)
- Bullet points for lists of services
- Bold key phrases for scanning
- No walls of text — every section should be scannable on mobile
