# About Page Copy Template

**Owner:** Raul | **Companion to:** `voice-and-copy-guide.md`, `services-page-copy-template.md`, `faq-copy-template.md`

The About page is the handshake. It's where a Phoenix homeowner decides whether the person behind the phone number is someone they'd let into their house / backyard / kitchen. Most small-biz About pages fail because they copy corporate "About Us" conventions — mission statements, generic history, stock team photos. Our template inverts that: specific story, real face, concrete proof, clear CTA.

## The 5-section skeleton

Every About page we ship has these sections, in this order, mobile-first.

1. **Hero: owner headshot + short headline**
2. **The story: how and why this business exists** (2–3 short paragraphs)
3. **What we stand for: 3 plain-English principles** (not values buzzwords)
4. **Proof: crew photo, certifications, service area, by-the-numbers stats**
5. **CTA: a warm invitation to call/book**

---

## Section 1 — Hero

**Layout:** Owner photo left (or top on mobile), short copy block right.

### Headline pattern
Use one of these three archetypes. Pick based on client's voice (Raul reads the voice.md before choosing):

- **Founder origin:** "I'm [First name]. I started [Business] in [year] after [specific trigger]."
- **Experience anchor:** "[Number] years of [service] in the Phoenix Valley. One truck. No shortcuts."
- **Family story:** "[Business] has been a [industry] shop in [neighborhood] since [year]. Three generations, same address."

### Subhead pattern
One sentence, plain, specific. Include Phoenix or a neighborhood.

**Good:** "We do pool service for 147 families across Ahwatukee, Chandler, and Tempe — and we'll tell you exactly what's wrong before we fix it."

**Bad:** "We strive to be the #1 pool service provider in Arizona through integrity, quality, and customer focus."

### Rules
- Hero should fit above the fold on a phone.
- Owner name + photo visible. If client is shy, negotiate hard — this single element drives more About-page conversion than anything else.
- No generic "Welcome to our website" copy. The visitor is here to vet the person, not the brand.

---

## Section 2 — The Story

2–3 paragraphs. Narrative, first-person if possible, third-person if the client prefers distance. Phoenix-local detail is required — at least one reference to a neighborhood, the heat, a Valley season, a local landmark, or the way AZ does business.

### Paragraph 1: Where it started
Answer the question: **why does this business exist?** Not "to serve customers" — what was the real moment? A broken washing machine their grandfather fixed? A bad experience with a national chain? Moving to Phoenix from somewhere else?

**Example:**
> "I fixed my first HVAC unit at 19, in my grandfather's garage in Maryvale. He'd been doing it since 1978 and he let me tag along every summer. When he retired in 2009, I took over the truck. Same name, same number, same promise: if we can't fix it in one visit, we'll tell you straight."

### Paragraph 2: What makes this different
Not a list of services — a point of view. What do they do that the Parker & Sons of the world don't?

**Example:**
> "Big AC companies in Phoenix run on commissions. Their techs get paid more when they sell you a new system. We're flat-rate and small on purpose — three trucks, one owner, and the guy diagnosing your unit doesn't get a bonus for telling you it's beyond repair."

### Paragraph 3 (optional): The present
Where the business is now. Size, team, service area. Keep it humble — "we're still small" is a trust signal in the trades.

**Example:**
> "Today it's me, my nephew Luis, and our dispatcher Maria. We cover Ahwatukee, Chandler, Tempe, and south Mesa, and we don't take jobs outside that radius — because if you call with an emergency, we want to be there in an hour, not three."

## Section 3 — What we stand for

Three principles. Each gets a short bolded line and one sentence of plain English.

### Anti-patterns (never ship these)
- "Integrity, Quality, Customer Service" — dead language
- "We strive to..." — strike "strive" forever
- "Passion for excellence" — meaningless
- Stock icons of handshakes / lightbulbs / trophies

### Pattern to use
Each principle = **a promise + the reason we can keep it.**

**Examples:**
- **Show up when we say we will.** One hour arrival window. We text you when the truck is 10 minutes out.
- **Tell you the cheapest fix first.** Most leaks don't need a full repipe. We'll explain the $40 option before the $4,000 one.
- **Clean up like we were never there.** Booties in the house, tarps on the lawn, drywall dust vacuumed, not blown around.

Three is the right number. Two feels thin, four feels like you're trying too hard.

## Section 4 — Proof

Visual + numeric + credential proof. Mixes images and stats.

### Elements (include at least 3)

- **Crew photo** — real, taken in Phoenix, on a real job site or at the client's shop. No studio shots.
- **By-the-numbers stat row** — 3 stats. Examples:
  - "Serving the Valley since 2009"
  - "2,847 pools maintained"
  - "4.9 stars on Google (218 reviews)"
- **Service area map** — Phoenix metro with pins on actual cities they cover
- **Credentials row** — AZ ROC #, Bonded, Insured, BBB, Angi Super Service (only the real ones)
- **Before/after gallery link** — 4–6 images if applicable (painters, landscapers, fencing, concrete)
- **Press / award mentions** — only if real. "Featured in Phoenix Magazine 2024" beats "Award-winning."

### Stat copy rules
- Specific numbers beat round numbers. "2,847" lands. "Over 2,000" feels padded.
- Lead with the year or duration. Phoenix customers weight longevity heavily.
- Reviews count + rating together. "4.9 / 218 reviews" > "4.9 stars."

---

## Section 5 — CTA

The About page is a trust checkpoint, not a dead end. End with a warm, specific invitation to take the next step.

### Copy patterns

**Pattern A — direct call:**
> "Ready to talk? Call [name] directly at [602-XXX-XXXX]. We answer the phone Mon–Sat, 7am–7pm."

**Pattern B — form with low commitment:**
> "Not ready to call? Send us a few details and we'll send back a ballpark quote — no visit required for most jobs."

**Pattern C — free audit / assessment (trades):**
> "Free 15-minute diagnostic. We'll tell you what's wrong, what it'll cost, and whether it's urgent — with zero pressure to book."

### Rules
- Always include a real phone number — not just a form.
- Put the CTA button + phone number side-by-side. Mobile users tap, desktop users copy.
- Echo one specific proof point in the CTA block ("Same-day service in Chandler," "Free quotes since 2009") — don't let the last thing they read be generic.

## Voice rules (pulled from voice-and-copy-guide.md)

- **Say "Phoenix" or the neighborhood name at least 3 times** across the About page. The page should feel unmistakably local.
- **Write at 6th–8th grade reading level.** Hemingway Editor check before ship.
- **Use contractions.** "We're," "don't," "won't" — formal language reads corporate and Phoenix trades don't respect corporate.
- **First-person if one owner, plural if team.** Pick and stick with it.
- **No exclamation points.** Never.
- **Heat reference is welcome, not required.** Don't force "beat the Arizona heat!" — but if it fits (HVAC, pool, landscaping, roofing), use it.
